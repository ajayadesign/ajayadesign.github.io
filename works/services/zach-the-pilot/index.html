Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about flight training? We've got answers.

The total cost varies based on how quickly you learn, but most students can expect to invest between $10,000–$15,000 for a Private Pilot License. This includes aircraft rental, instructor fees, ground school materials, and exam fees. We'll create a personalized estimate during your first consultation.
On average, students complete their PPL in 3–6 months when flying 2–3 times per week. The FAA requires a minimum of 40 flight hours, though the national average is around 60–70 hours. Your pace depends on consistency, weather, and personal aptitude.
Absolutely not! Everyone starts somewhere. All you need is the desire to fly. You must be at least 16 to solo and 17 to earn your private pilot certificate. A valid medical certificate from an Aviation Medical Examiner (AME) is also required.
We train in well-maintained Cessna 172s and Piper PA-28s — among the most popular training aircraft worldwide. These aircraft are stable, forgiving, and perfect for students. All aircraft are IFR-equipped for instrument training.
Safety always comes first. If weather conditions aren't suitable for flying, we'll reschedule at no extra charge. On weather days, we can often use the time for productive ground school instead — covering weather theory, flight planning, or exam preparation.
