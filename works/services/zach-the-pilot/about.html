Meet Your Instructor: Zach Seufer

From my very first discovery flight, I knew aviation was my calling. There's nothing quite like the feeling of breaking through a cloud layer at sunrise or the quiet hum of an engine at cruise altitude. That passion for flight is what I bring to every lesson.

As an FAA Certified Flight Instructor (CFI), I've had the privilege of guiding dozens of students from their first nervous taxi to their triumphant checkride. My teaching philosophy is simple: patient, thorough, and safety-first instruction that builds confident aviators.

I'm currently building hours toward my dream of becoming a commercial airline pilot. This means my students benefit from an instructor who is actively flying, constantly learning, and deeply immersed in the world of aviation every single day.

My Aviation Journey

Every pilot has a story — here's how mine is unfolding.

Discovery Flight

Took my first discovery flight and instantly fell in love with aviation. The freedom and perspective from above changed everything.

Private Pilot License

Earned my PPL and realized I wanted to share the joy of flight with others. Started dreaming of a career in aviation.

Instrument Rating & CFI

Completed instrument training and earned my CFI certificate. Began teaching students and building toward 1,500 hours for the ATP.

The Road to Airlines

Currently building flight hours, training students, and working toward my Airline Transport Pilot (ATP) certificate — the final step to the flight deck.

Teaching Philosophy

What makes training with Zach different.

Safety Above All

Aeronautical Decision Making (ADM) and risk management are woven into every lesson. I train pilots who know when to fly — and when not to.

Patience & Encouragement

Learning to fly is challenging. I meet every student where they are and build confidence through positive reinforcement and clear communication.

Scenario-Based Training

Real-world scenarios, not just maneuvers. I prepare students for the decisions they'll face as Pilot in Command, not just the checkride.
