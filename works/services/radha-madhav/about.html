Our Mission

Inspiring Devotion to
Radha Krishna

The path of Bhakti (devotion) is a soul's journey toward Radha Krishna. Our mission is to inspire you to begin that journey, and if you are fortunate to have begun that journey, we hope to inspire you to make steady progress and reach your goal.

Radha Madhav Dham is one of the largest Hindu temples and ashrams in the U.S. Built in 1990, it was created to preserve the devotional environment that prevailed in the ancient ashrams of Vrindavan. Spread over 200 acres in the Hill Country of Austin, this ashram is a representation of the holy land of Braj in India where Radha Rani and Shree Krishna appeared 5,000 years ago.

All souls, regardless of their faith and belief, are welcome at Radha Madhav Dham. We hope you are inspired to learn Hindu philosophy and follow the path of Bhakti that is practiced here.

The Temple

Nikunj Darshan of
Radha Krishna

Shree Raseshwari Radha Rani Temple is the largest temple of Radha Rani in the world. It is designed to create a vision of Radha Krishna in nikunj darshan as described by Rasik Saints.

Radha Krishna are portrayed sitting, standing, or playing in an open space surrounded by flowering gardens, fragrant atmosphere, and river Yamuna running behind. You also see Raseshwari Radha Rani sitting on the throne, surrounded by flowering gardens and a rasik saint absorbed in Their divine darshan.

Sacred Grounds

Holy Landmarks of Braj

Of the several landmarks represented in the 200+ acre property, some are most popularly visited by guests. Take a walking tour in loving remembrance of Krishna leelas that took place in these holy places.

Govardhan Hill

Recalls the Krishna leela where Little Kanha held up this hill on His little finger for seven days to protect all the Brajwasis from Lord Indra's torrential rains.

Prem Sarovar

The "Pond of Divine Love" where hundreds of loving, joyful Radha Krishna leelas were performed.

Barsana Hill

Reminiscent of the place atop a small hill in Barsana Village where Radha Rani's palace was situated. Today, Shreeji Temple is built on this spot.

Raas Mandal

Celebrates the Raas leela of Radha Krishna, showcased by the outdoor shrine with Radha, Krishna, and 8 Maha Sakhis performing Raas.

Radha Kund

Marvel at the divine beauty of this sacred kund, a representation of the holiest bathing place in all of Braj.

Kali Dah

Reminds us of the time when Krishna danced on Kaliya Naag and forced him away from the Yamuna river, clearing Yamunaji from his poison.

Natural Beauty
